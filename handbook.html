Welcome to Caelis Air

Caelis Air is a community-first virtual airline built for flight simulation enthusiasts of all experience levels. We are not here to tell you what aircraft to fly, where to fly, or how long your flights need to be. We are here to bring aviation enthusiasts together.

Whether you fly airliners, general aviation, helicopters, military aircraft, vintage aircraft, or anything in between, you have a place here.

At Caelis Air, every journey matters.

Our Philosophy

Caelis Air is built around one simple idea:

Core IdeaFly what you want. When you want. Where you want.

Unlike traditional virtual airlines, Caelis Air does not require members to follow a fixed fleet, route network, or schedule. We believe flight simulation should be flexible, welcoming, and fun.

You can fly a short VFR hop in a Cessna, a long-haul airline route in a 777, a helicopter mission, a fighter jet sortie, or a casual sightseeing flight. Your simulator experience is your own — and we are here to share it with you.

Who We Are

Caelis Air is a virtual aviation community focused on:

  • Freedom to fly your way
  • Community events and group flights
  • Screenshots, stories, and shared journeys
  • Welcoming pilots of every skill level
  • Civilian and Special Operations-style liveries
  • A positive, relaxed, and aviation-focused environment

We are not a real airline and are not affiliated with any real-world airline, military branch, aircraft manufacturer, or government organization. Caelis Air exists for entertainment, flight simulation, and community purposes only.

Divisions

Caelis Air Civil Division

The Civil Division represents the main Caelis Air community identity.

This division includes aircraft such as:

  • Airbus A320neo
  • Boeing 737-800
  • Boeing 777-300ER
  • Boeing 747-400
  • Cessna 172
  • Other civilian aircraft members choose to fly

Civil Division flights can include airline operations, general aviation, sightseeing, bush flying, long-haul flights, cargo flights, business aviation, and casual free flights.

TaglineFly Far. Fly Together.

Caelis Air Special Operations

Caelis Air Special Operations, also known as CA-SOG, is the military-inspired side of Caelis Air for members who enjoy special mission, fighter, helicopter, and stealth aircraft flying in simulators.

This division includes aircraft such as:

  • F-22 Raptor
  • F-35 Lightning II
  • B-2 Spirit
  • UH-60 Black Hawk
  • Other military or special mission aircraft used in supported simulators

Caelis Air Special Operations is fictional and exists only for flight simulation, roleplay, screenshots, events, and community activities.

MottoPrecision. Unity. Excellence.

Membership

Anyone with an interest in aviation or flight simulation is welcome to join Caelis Air.

You do not need to be an expert pilot. You do not need to fly on VATSIM. You do not need to own a specific simulator or aircraft. You simply need to respect the community and share a love for aviation.

New members are encouraged to introduce themselves, ask questions, share screenshots, and join events whenever they feel comfortable.

Supported Flying Styles

Caelis Air welcomes many styles of flying, including:

  • Airline operations
  • General aviation
  • Bush flying
  • Helicopter operations
  • Military-style simulation
  • Long-haul flights
  • Short hops
  • Cargo flights
  • VFR sightseeing
  • IFR practice
  • Group flights
  • Offline flights
  • Online network flights

There is no single correct way to enjoy Caelis Air.

Flight Logging

Caelis Air focuses on journeys, not restrictions.

Members may share flights through screenshots, journey posts, event participation, or any future tracking system used by the community.

A flight log may include:

  • Departure airport
  • Arrival airport
  • Aircraft flown
  • Simulator used
  • Flight time
  • Screenshots
  • Route notes
  • Favorite moment from the flight

Short flights and long flights are both welcome. A 20-minute sightseeing flight matters just as much as a 12-hour ocean crossing.

Events

Caelis Air events are designed to bring members together without limiting creativity.

Event types may include:

  • Friday Night Fly-Outs
  • Destination of the Week
  • Screenshot Sunday
  • Bush Trip Weekends
  • Historic Aircraft Nights
  • Around the World Challenges
  • Helicopter Operations
  • Special Operations Missions
  • Long-Haul Community Flights
  • Seasonal and holiday events

Unless an event says otherwise, members are encouraged to fly the aircraft they enjoy most.

Community Standards

Caelis Air is built on respect, kindness, and inclusion.

All members are expected to:

  • Treat others with respect
  • Welcome new pilots
  • Avoid gatekeeping
  • Keep discussions friendly
  • Use channels appropriately
  • Follow Discord's Terms of Service
  • Avoid harassment, hate speech, threats, scams, or offensive content
  • Listen to staff when moderation is needed

We want Caelis Air to be a place where people feel comfortable learning, sharing, and flying together.

Screenshots and Media

Members are encouraged to share screenshots, videos, clips, liveries, flight stories, and simulator moments.

When sharing media:

  • Keep content appropriate
  • Credit creators when needed
  • Do not steal or repost someone else's work as your own
  • Avoid real-world political or sensitive military messaging
  • Keep fictional Caelis branding respectful and community-focused

Screenshots and media are a major part of the Caelis Air experience.

Liveries

Caelis Air uses two main livery styles:

Civil Livery

The Civil livery features a clean white base, Midnight Navy, Sky Blue accents, and the Caelis Air logo. It is designed to feel modern, welcoming, and recognizable.

Special Operations Livery

The Special Operations livery uses Gunmetal Gray, Midnight Navy, Slate Gray, Steel Blue, and Muted Silver. It is designed to look low-visibility, professional, and military-inspired while remaining fictional.

Members may use official Caelis Air liveries when available or create their own, as long as they respect the brand and community guidelines.

Ranks and Recognition

Caelis Air does not restrict aircraft access by rank.

Instead of using ranks to limit what you can fly, Caelis Air may use recognition roles, badges, or achievements to celebrate community involvement.

Examples may include:

  • New Explorer
  • Pathfinder
  • Voyager
  • Wayfinder
  • Sky Ambassador
  • Horizon Keeper

Recognition is based on participation, helpfulness, events, creativity, and community spirit — not just flight hours.
